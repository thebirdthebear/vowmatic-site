Getting Started

Install Vowmatic

Open Terminal on your Mac (search "Terminal" in Spotlight) and paste this command:

curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/thebirdthebear/wedding-analyzer/main/install.sh | bash

This downloads Vowmatic and sets up your free trial (1 wedding, all features). A Vowmatic shortcut appears on your Desktop.

Run your first wedding

  1. Double-click Vowmatic on your Desktop
  2. A browser window opens. Click Choose Folder and select your wedding footage folder
  3. Select your NLE (Final Cut Pro or DaVinci Resolve) and choose Full Analysis mode
  4. Click Start — Vowmatic scans and classifies all clips (~10 min depending on footage volume)
  5. Review the Clip Review tab to fix any misclassified clips, then set up Audio for each event
  6. Hit Continue — sync, autocut, ratings, and story assembly run unattended (~30 min)
  7. Import the generated wedding_combined.fcpxml (FCP) or per-event files (Resolve) into your NLE

What you get in your NLE

After import, you'll find every clip classified by event keyword (ceremony, toasts, first look, portraits, getting ready, etc.) with favorites marked, low-quality clips flagged, and multicam autocut timelines for ceremony and toasts ready to fine-tune.

Upgrading from free trial

After your first wedding, visit pricing to purchase credits or a subscription. Then re-run the install command with your token:

curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/thebirdthebear/wedding-analyzer/main/install.sh | bash -s -- --token YOUR_TOKEN

Your token is emailed to you after purchase. This replaces the trial token — all your existing project data is preserved.

Common Questions

What NLEs are supported?

Final Cut Pro and DaVinci Resolve. Each gets native-format output optimized for that editor. Premiere Pro support is in progress.

What cameras work?

Anything that shoots MP4 or MOV. Tested with Sony (A7, FX, A1), Canon (R5, R6), DJI drones, and GoPro. Audio supports MixPre, Zoom, and standard WAV files.

Does my footage get uploaded?

No. All video and audio stays on your Mac. Only small frame thumbnails (~100KB each) are sent to AI for clip classification. Nothing is stored or used for training.

How long does processing take?

About 45 minutes total, mostly unattended. The initial scan takes ~10 minutes and needs a brief review. The rest runs without you.

Can I adjust the autocut after?

The autocut imports as a normal multicam timeline in your NLE. Blade through it, swap angles, trim cuts — it's a starting point, not a locked edit.

What macOS version do I need?

macOS 12 (Monterey) or newer. Apple Silicon (M1/M2/M3/M4/M5) recommended for fastest Whisper transcription. Intel Macs work too.

Do credits expire?

Never. Buy a credit today, use it whenever you need it.

How do I update Vowmatic?

Re-run the same install command. It downloads the latest version and preserves your settings and token.

Troubleshooting

Vowmatic won't launch

Make sure you're on macOS 12+. Try re-running the install command to get the latest version. If the Desktop shortcut doesn't work, open Terminal and run: cd ~/vowmatic && python3 launcher.py

Clips not linking in FCP / Resolve

Make sure your footage drive is connected before importing the FCPXML. Vowmatic references your original file paths — if the drive letter or mount point changed, you'll need to relink manually in your NLE.

"API error" during classification

This usually means a network issue. Check your internet connection and try again. If it persists, your token may have expired — email us and we'll check your account.

Wrong event classifications

Use the Clip Review tab to reclassify any misidentified clips before continuing to sync. Your corrections are saved and used for the rest of the pipeline.

Report a Bug

Found something broken? We want to hear about it. When reporting, please include:

  1. What happened — describe the issue and which step of the pipeline you were on
  2. What you expected — what should have happened instead
  3. Camera/audio gear — camera model(s), audio recorder, file formats
  4. Terminal output — copy any error messages from the Terminal window (scroll up if needed)

Screenshots and screen recordings are extremely helpful. Send everything to the email below.
