Help & Support

Everything you need to get started, troubleshoot, and get the most out of Vowmatic.

Install Vowmatic

Download Vowmatic (includes everything you need).

  1. Open the downloaded Vowmatic.dmg
  2. Drag Vowmatic to your Applications folder
  3. Double-click Vowmatic to launch — your first wedding is free, no account needed

Note: On first launch, macOS may show a "developer cannot be verified" warning. Right-click the app and choose "Open" to bypass this. This only happens once.

Run Your First Wedding

  1. Launch Vowmatic from your Applications folder
  2. A browser window opens. Click Choose Folder and pick the folder where your wedding footage lives
  3. Choose your editing software (Final Cut Pro, DaVinci Resolve, or Premiere Pro) and select Full Analysis
  4. Click Start — Vowmatic scans your footage and sorts every clip by event (~10 minutes)
  5. A review screen opens. Quickly check that clips are sorted correctly, then tell it which audio files go with which events (ceremony mic, toast mic, etc.)
  6. Click Continue — Vowmatic syncs your cameras, builds multicam edits, and organizes everything. Go grab coffee — this takes about 30 minutes and runs on its own.
  7. When it's done, open the files it created in your editing software. Everything is ready to go.

Your Editor

Select your NLE below for a walkthrough video and import instructions specific to your editor.

Walkthrough

What you'll get

Vowmatic creates an FCPXML bundle that opens directly in Final Cut Pro:

Selects library

All your clips sorted into keyword collections by event (ceremony, toasts, portraits, getting ready, etc.). Favorites are marked. Shaky or unusable clips are flagged.

Multicam edits

A multicam timeline for each event — ceremony, toasts, and more. All cameras are synced and Vowmatic has already made the cut decisions for you. Use the Angle Viewer to switch angles.

Story edit

An AI-assembled highlight reel pulling the best moments from your ceremony and toasts. This is your starting point for the final film.

Importing

  1. Open Final Cut Pro
  2. Go to File → Import → XML and select the .fcpxml file from your project folder
  3. FCP will create a new event with all selects, multicam timelines, and story edits organized inside
  4. Open a multicam timeline and use Window → Show in Workspace → Angle Viewer to switch cameras

Walkthrough

What you'll get

Vowmatic creates three XML files. Import them via File → Import:

premiere_selects.xml

All your clips organized into bins by event (ceremony, toasts, portraits, etc.) with favorites marked. This is your clip library — import this first.

premiere_multitrack.xml

A sequence for each event with the autocut rough cut on the top video track and all synced cameras on the tracks below. Blade through the autocut to reveal the camera you want.

premiere_story.xml

The AI-assembled story edit with the best angle on top and all synced cameras below — same multi-track layout. This is your starting point for the highlight film.

Importing

  1. Open Premiere Pro and create or open your project
  2. File → Import → select premiere_selects.xml first — brings in all clips organized into bins by event, with favorites marked
  3. File → Import → select premiere_multitrack.xml — each event appears as a sequence with the autocut on the top track and synced cameras below
  4. File → Import → select premiere_story.xml for the AI-assembled story edit
  5. If prompted to relink media, click Locate, find one clip on your drive, and check Relink others automatically

Tips

Audio: Board audio is active on all sequences. Lav mics (groom, officiant) are imported but muted — unmute them if you prefer lav audio.

Multicam switching: To get Premiere's native multicam angle switching, select the synced camera clips on the lower tracks, right-click, and choose Create Multi-Camera Source Sequence. Then use the multicam viewer to switch angles in real time.

Troubleshooting

Media shows as offline: Your footage drive wasn't connected when you imported, or the drive letter changed. Go to File → Link Media, click Locate on any clip, navigate to it on your drive, and check "Relink others automatically."

Autocut track shows wrong angle: Make sure you imported premiere_multitrack.xml (not the selects file). The autocut should be on the top video track.

Walkthrough

What you'll get

Vowmatic creates FCPXML files optimized for DaVinci Resolve:

Selects library

All your clips sorted into bins by event (ceremony, toasts, portraits, getting ready, etc.). Favorites are marked. Shaky or unusable clips are flagged.

Multicam edits

A multicam timeline for each event with synced cameras and Vowmatic's suggested cuts. Switch angles in the timeline or use Resolve's multicam viewer.

Story edit

An AI-assembled highlight reel — your starting point for the final film.

Importing

  1. Open DaVinci Resolve and create or open a project
  2. Go to File → Import → Timeline (or File → Import AAF, EDL, XML)
  3. Select the .fcpxml file from your project folder
  4. Resolve will import the selects, multicam timelines, and story edit
  5. If media appears offline, right-click a clip and choose Relink Selected Clips, then navigate to your footage drive

Tips

Timeline settings: Make sure your project timeline framerate matches your footage (typically 23.976 or 29.97) before importing.

Multicam: You can switch angles directly in the Edit page timeline, or use Resolve's multicam viewer for real-time switching.

Upgrading After Your Free Wedding

After your first wedding, visit pricing to purchase credits or a subscription. We'll email you a personal license key. To activate it, open Terminal on your Mac and paste the command from your email. It looks like this:

python3 -c "import json; from pathlib import Path; p=Path.home()/'.wedding_analyzer/config.json'; d=json.loads(p.read_text()); d['api_token']='YOUR_KEY_HERE'; p.write_text(json.dumps(d,indent=2)); print('Done')"

Your email will have this command with your key already filled in — just copy, paste, done. All your existing work is saved.

Common Questions

What NLEs are supported?

Final Cut Pro, DaVinci Resolve, and Premiere Pro. Each gets native-format output optimized for that editor.

What cameras work?

Anything that shoots MP4 or MOV. Tested with Sony (A7, FX, A1), Canon (R5, R6), DJI drones, and GoPro. Audio supports MixPre, Zoom, and standard WAV files.

Does my footage get uploaded?

No. All video and audio stays on your Mac. Only small frame thumbnails (~100KB each) are sent to AI for clip classification. Nothing is stored or used for training.

How long does processing take?

About 45 minutes total, mostly unattended. The initial scan takes ~10 minutes and needs a brief review. The rest runs without you.

Can I change the edit after?

Absolutely. Everything opens as a normal timeline in your editor. Move cuts around, swap camera angles, trim — it's a starting point, not a final edit.

What Mac do I need?

macOS 12 (Monterey) or newer. Works on any Mac, but Apple Silicon (M1+) is faster. Intel Macs work too.

Do credits expire?

Never. Buy a credit today, use it whenever you need it.

How do I update Vowmatic?

Download the latest DMG from our download page and replace the app in your Applications folder. Your settings and project data are preserved.

Troubleshooting

Vowmatic won't launch

Make sure you're on macOS 12+. If macOS blocks the app with "cannot be verified," right-click the app and choose Open (this only happens once). If issues persist, try re-downloading from our download page.

Clips not showing up in your editor

Make sure your footage drive is plugged in before opening the file Vowmatic created. It links to your original footage — if the drive isn't connected or was renamed, your editor won't find the clips. You can relink them manually.

Getting an error during scanning

This usually means your internet connection dropped. Vowmatic needs internet for the clip-sorting step. Check your connection and try again. If it keeps happening, email us.

Some clips got sorted into the wrong category

That happens sometimes — AI isn't perfect. Use the Clip Review screen to drag misidentified clips to the right category. Your changes are saved and used for everything that comes after.
