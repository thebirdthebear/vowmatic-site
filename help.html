Getting Started

Install Vowmatic

Download Vowmatic.dmg (includes everything you need).

  1. Open the downloaded Vowmatic.dmg
  2. Drag Vowmatic to your Applications folder
  3. Double-click Vowmatic to launch — your first wedding is free, no account needed

Note: On first launch, macOS may show a "developer cannot be verified" warning. Right-click the app and choose "Open" to bypass this. This only happens once.

Run your first wedding

  1. Launch Vowmatic from your Applications folder
  2. A browser window opens. Click Choose Folder and pick the folder where your wedding footage lives
  3. Choose your editing software (Final Cut Pro or DaVinci Resolve) and select Full Analysis
  4. Click Start — Vowmatic scans your footage and sorts every clip by event (~10 minutes)
  5. A review screen opens. Quickly check that clips are sorted correctly, then tell it which audio files go with which events (ceremony mic, toast mic, etc.)
  6. Click Continue — Vowmatic syncs your cameras, builds multicam edits, and organizes everything. Go grab coffee — this takes about 30 minutes and runs on its own.
  7. When it's done, open the file it created in your editing software. Everything is ready to go.

What you'll find in your editor

Every clip sorted into categories you can browse (ceremony, toasts, first look, portraits, getting ready, and more). Your best clips are marked as favorites. Shaky or unusable ones are flagged. And you get rough-cut timelines for your ceremony, toasts, and other events — ready to fine-tune.

Upgrading after your free wedding

After your first wedding, visit pricing to purchase credits or a subscription. We'll email you a personal license key. To activate it, open Terminal on your Mac and paste the command from your email. It looks like this:

python3 -c "import json; from pathlib import Path; p=Path.home()/'.wedding_analyzer/config.json'; d=json.loads(p.read_text()); d['api_token']='YOUR_KEY_HERE'; p.write_text(json.dumps(d,indent=2)); print('Done')"

Your email will have this command with your key already filled in — just copy, paste, done. All your existing work is saved.

Common Questions

What NLEs are supported?

Final Cut Pro and DaVinci Resolve. Each gets native-format output optimized for that editor. Premiere Pro support is in progress.

What cameras work?

Anything that shoots MP4 or MOV. Tested with Sony (A7, FX, A1), Canon (R5, R6), DJI drones, and GoPro. Audio supports MixPre, Zoom, and standard WAV files.

Does my footage get uploaded?

No. All video and audio stays on your Mac. Only small frame thumbnails (~100KB each) are sent to AI for clip classification. Nothing is stored or used for training.

How long does processing take?

About 45 minutes total, mostly unattended. The initial scan takes ~10 minutes and needs a brief review. The rest runs without you.

Can I change the multicam edit after?

Absolutely. The multicam edit opens as a normal timeline in your editor. Move cuts around, swap camera angles, trim — it's a starting point, not a final edit.

What Mac do I need?

macOS 12 (Monterey) or newer. Works on any Mac, but Apple Silicon (M1 and newer) is faster. Intel Macs work too — just a bit slower on some steps.

Do credits expire?

Never. Buy a credit today, use it whenever you need it.

How do I update Vowmatic?

Download the latest DMG from our releases page and replace the app in your Applications folder. Your settings and project data are preserved.

Troubleshooting

Vowmatic won't launch

Make sure you're on macOS 12+. If macOS blocks the app with "cannot be verified," right-click the app and choose Open (this only happens once). If issues persist, try re-downloading the latest DMG.

Clips not showing up in FCP / Resolve

Make sure your footage drive is plugged in before opening the file Vowmatic created. It links to your original footage — if the drive isn't connected or was renamed, your editor won't find the clips. You can relink them manually in your editor.

Getting an error during scanning

This usually means your internet connection dropped. Vowmatic needs internet for the clip-sorting step. Check your connection and try again. If it keeps happening, email us — we'll help.

Some clips got sorted into the wrong category

That happens sometimes — AI isn't perfect. Use the Clip Review screen to drag misidentified clips to the right category. Your changes are saved and used for everything that comes after.
